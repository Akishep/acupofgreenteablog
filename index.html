Bathing Tea

"Mr.Frog, how is the temprature of your bath?"
「お湯加減はいかが。」

This bright moss green pond of the allotment we visited the other day reminds me of Macha. In Japan, unless you learn how to make tea or visit Kyoto, you would not have a bowl of macha in your daily life. Instead, I often drink Japanese green tea, which is paler green tea called 'Sencha'. Not only the colour but the taste is also lighter than Macha. This tea is very simple to make unlike Macha. Only matter is the temperature of the hot water. It is supposed to be around 70 ° to 80 ° in order to get more flavour.It is simply waiting for 10 mins or so until the temperature becomes right, but often I am not very patient. The kettle sounds like a weeping child. Then I remember the quote by my respected Japanese critic, "Having tea is a punctuation of your daily life."

　近所のアロットメント（共有菜園）が公開されていて、そこで見つけた小さなお池。 こっくりとした濃厚な抹茶色に魅せられたのは私だけではなく、同じように魅せられた蛙を見つけて子供たちは大はしゃぎ。
京都に行くと二寧坂の甘味処「かさぎ屋」の薄茶を目指すくらいで、私は普段から抹茶を飲むことはない。抹茶色の池に反応しても、抹茶とはその程度の付き合いだ。毎日私が飲むお茶と言えば、専ら煎茶なのだけれど、まるで泣き喚く子供を宥めるように、シュンシュンと沸き立つ薬缶を見つめて、お湯が適温まで下がっていくのをじっと待つ。私の敬愛する批評家の言葉を思い出しながら。「お茶とは日常の句読点を打つということ。」

Deep Fried Chiken Recipe - Karaage

"How do you make Japanese fried chiken?"

I will introduce a very simple recipe of deep fried chicken with Japanese style (Karaage). Originally this dish came from China in early 17c. As Japanese are very good at making a copy and adding more or less, it became one of the most popular dishes by people of all ages. It is the No.1 request from my kids. Enjoy! Itadakimasu- Thank you, let's eat!


Two tips:

- Use the potato starch instead of plain flour to fry that you can get from any oriental shop.

- Use a good amount of grounded ginger and garlic

Ingredients:
(For two)

  • Chicken thigh 500g
  • (A)
  • Soy sauce 3 Tablespoon
  • Ginger 1 Tablespoon
  • Sake (Ideally) 2 Tablespoon
  • Potato Starch 6 Tablespoon
  • Lemon if you like

Methods:
  • 1. Cut into the pieces of chicken and put them and ingredients(A) in a bag or container for 30 minutes at least.
  • 2.  Sprinkle potato starches around the chicken pieces.  Then fry them for 4 minutes at 180 degrees, then turn up the heat to 200 degrees at the last minute.
